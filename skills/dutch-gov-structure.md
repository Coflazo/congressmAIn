# Dutch Municipal Government — Quick Reference

Use this as context when processing council meeting transcripts.

## Structure

### Gemeenteraad (Municipal Council)
- Elected body that makes decisions for the municipality
- Members are called **raadsleden** (councillors), organised by **fractie** (party group)
- The council votes on proposals, budgets, regulations (verordeningen), and zoning plans (bestemmingsplannen)
- Led by the **voorzitter** (chair), usually the burgemeester (mayor)

### College van B&W (Executive Board)
- The **burgemeester** (mayor) + **wethouders** (aldermen/portfolio holders)
- Wethouders propose policy and execute decisions made by the council
- Each wethouder has a portfolio (e.g., housing, transport, sustainability)

### Key roles in a meeting
- **Voorzitter** — chairs the meeting, manages debate, calls votes
- **Wethouder** — presents proposals, answers questions from the council
- **Raadslid** — asks questions, proposes amendments/motions, votes
- **Griffier** — clerk, manages meeting administration (rarely speaks)

## Types of decisions

### Voorstel (Proposal)
A proposal from the college to the council. The council votes to accept or reject it.

### Amendement (Amendment)
A change to a proposal, submitted by one or more council members. Voted on before the final proposal vote. If accepted, the proposal is modified.

### Motie (Motion)
A statement or request from the council to the college. Not legally binding but politically significant. Can request investigation, policy change, or evaluation.

### Verordening (Regulation/Bylaw)
A local law. More formal than a motion. Once passed, it has legal force.

### Bestemmingsplan (Zoning Plan)
Determines what land can be used for (housing, commercial, green space, etc.). Changes require council approval.

## Voting

- Votes are usually by show of hands or head count
- Results are reported as: **voor** (for), **tegen** (against), **onthoudingen** (abstentions)
- Votes are often reported by party, not individual
- A simple majority (>50% of votes cast) is needed to pass
- **Unaniem** = unanimous (all in favour)
